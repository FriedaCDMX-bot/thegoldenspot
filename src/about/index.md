I'm a high school senior living in Newport Beach, California, and this blog
is my corner of the internet for sharing everything that helps me feel my
absolute best: food, movement, and every-day routines.

I'm especially interested by nutrition, whether it's the science or the way
nutrition interacts with psychology. Intuitive eating is a concept I became
exposed to a couple of years ago, and have since adopted into my own food
philosophy. I'm not a fan of diet culture. I don't think it works, healthily,
for me or for most people. What I aim for instead is an ideal middle ground:
the unique combination of eating, moving, sleeping, and living by your values
that makes *you* feel your best. It's different for everyone, and that's the
whole point.

> Everyone is unique in what they need and enjoy — and that's the delicate balance I aim for.

## What you'll find here

- **Food** — nutritious meal inspiration I actually eat, kept as a recipe book you can filter.
- **Movement** — my current fav ways to move my body, whether that's yoga, mat pilates, weightlifting, or walking, done for strength and joy.
- **Thoughts** — early mornings, sleep, current favorites, and what's been making me feel my best lately.
- **The Deep Dive** — occasional longer, sourced articles on nutrition science, wellness trend myth-busting, or anything wellness-related.
- **Favorites** — products, outfits, and wishlist finds I'm loving right now.

I'm writing as a student, for students. So, if you're in high school or
college and want something relatable *and* informative, you're exactly who I
had in mind.

Everything here is personal experience plus student research. I cite sources
where I can, but this isn't medical or dietetic advice. For personal health
questions, please talk to a qualified professional.
