# Izzy's Busy — Brand Strategy

*Working strategy document. Drafted July 2026.*

## Positioning statement

A nutrition-and-wellness blog written **by a student, for students** — the peer voice
that most anti-diet-culture content is missing. Most intuitive-eating and wellness
blogs are written by people in their 30s and 40s; readers in high school and college
get either that, or toxic trend content on social media. This blog sits in the gap:
relatable daily life + short, evidence-based writing, from someone actually living
the reader's stage of life.

**One-line pitch:** *Feel your absolute best — no diet culture required.*

## Core philosophy (the "why")

- Against diet culture: it isn't a sustainable or healthy lifestyle for most people.
- For whatever makes *you* feel your best: intuitive eating, exercising to feel fit
  and strong (not to shrink), sleep, living by your values.
- Everyone's "golden spot" is unique — the blog helps readers find theirs, it doesn't
  prescribe one.

## Voice

- **Older-sister energy:** warm, honest, encouraging — never preachy or clinical.
- **Evidence-grounded:** short articles cite real sources; myth-busting is done with
  curiosity, not superiority.
- **Honestly in-progress:** the writer is learning too. "Here's what's working for me
  and what the research says" — never "here's what you must do."

## Audience

High school and college students seeking inspiration and like-mindedness. They come
for meal inspiration that's actually eaten (not staged), realistic routines, and
straight answers about wellness trends. After six months of reading, they should
have a calmer relationship with food and movement and a routine that fits *them*.

## Content pillars

1. **Food & food relationships** — real meal inspiration, intuitive-eating
   reflections, personal viewpoints on food. (Photo-driven + short reflections.)
2. **Movement** — yoga, mat pilates, weightlifting, daily movement; training to feel
   strong. (Lists/guides + reflections.)
3. **Routines & feeling your best** — early mornings, sleep, values-driven habits,
   "what's been making me feel my best lately." (Short reflections.)
4. **The Deep Dive (occasional column)** — longer, referenced, evidence-based posts:
   nutrition science explained simply, current-trend myth-busting. (Monthly-ish.)

## Format & cadence

- Mostly short-form: photo-driven posts, lists/guides, short reflections.
- 1–2 short posts per week is plenty; the Deep Dive column roughly monthly.
- Consistency beats volume — especially during college application season.

## Editorial guardrails

Because the audience is young and the topic is food/body-adjacent:

- Never publish calorie counts, weight-loss framing, or before/after content.
- Deep Dive posts cite sources (studies, registered dietitians, reputable orgs).
- A standing disclaimer: this is personal experience + student research, not medical
  or dietetic advice.

These guardrails are also a differentiator — and for college applications, they
demonstrate rigor and responsibility, not just enthusiasm.

## Goals

- Primary: creative outlet with a consistent publishing habit.
- Near-term: a meaningful, polished project to reference in college applications.
- Later (revisit in ~1 year): possible platform growth / monetization — not now.

## Name

**Izzy's Busy** (chosen July 2026; previously launched under the working title
"The Golden Spot"). "The golden spot" survives as the blog's signature phrase
for its core philosophy — everyone's unique feel-your-best combination.
