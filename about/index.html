Hi, I’m glad you’re here.

I’m a student living near the coast in Southern California, and this blog is my little corner of the internet for everything that helps me feel my absolute best: food I genuinely enjoy, movement that makes me feel strong, and routines that keep me grounded.

I’m especially fascinated by nutrition and how it intersects with psychology — things like intuitive eating and why our relationships with food matter as much as the food itself. I’m not a fan of diet culture. I don’t think it works, healthily, for me or for most people. What I aim for instead is what I call the golden spot: the unique combination of eating, moving, sleeping, and living by your values that makes you feel your best. It’s different for everyone, and that’s the whole point.

Everyone is unique in what they need and enjoy — and that’s the golden spot I aim for.

What you’ll find here

  • Food — meal inspiration I actually use, and honest reflections on food relationships.
  • Movement — yoga, mat pilates, weightlifting, and everyday movement, done for strength and joy.
  • Routines — early mornings, sleep, and what’s been making me feel my best lately.
  • The Deep Dive — occasional longer, sourced articles on nutrition science and wellness trend myth-busting.

Most blogs in this space are written by people a couple of decades older than their readers. I’m writing as a student, for students — so if you’re in high school or college and want something relatable and informative, you’re exactly who I had in mind.

Everything here is personal experience plus student research — I cite sources where I can, but this isn’t medical or dietetic advice. For personal health questions, please talk to a qualified professional.
